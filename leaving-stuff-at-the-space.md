# Leaving Stuff at the Space

> **In general, we ask that people refrain from leaving items at the space without approval.**
{.is-warning}

Items left at the space should either be **colocated**, **donated**, or have a **green parking pass**.

Items left at the space without approval may be absorbed by the space and/or disposed of without warning.
There are two main reasons for this:
1. Every hackerspace eventually gets full of junk. We're tiny as it is and want to make sure the space we do have serves everyone as best as possible.
2. We can't tell who owns what if things are just left at the space.

## Donating items

We accept tax-deductable donations of physical items! However, we still want to make sure that those items benefit the space. If you would like to donate a physical item, please reach out to the [Secretary](/the-board).

## Colocating items

Tools and appliances may be colocated at the space. These items remain owned by whoever is colocating them but are available for use by everyone at PawPrint. Colocation may be ended at any time (although notice is appreciated). If you would like to colocate an item, please reach out to the [Secretary](/the-board).

## Parking Passes

In-progress projects may live at the space temporarily if they get a parking pass.

How to request a parking pass:
1. Obtain a yellow Parking Request slip from the office. They usually live below the office computer monitor.
2. Fill out the Parking Request slip and tape it to the object that you wish to store at the space.
3. Wait for a board member to issue a green Parking Pass for the item. In the meantime, the item may be left at the space.
4. If the item is not suitable for long-term storage, a red Parking Ticket may be issued instead of a green Parking Pass.

### Red Parking Tickets

A red parking ticket may be issued for items left at the space, with an expiration date at the discression of the ticketer (usually a few weeks).

Red-ticket items are documented in the [Parking Pass Enforcement Telegram channel](https://t.me/+WWdVcpVSjN83YTFh).  We recommend you keep notifications on so lost items may be reunited with their rightful owner.

After a red ticket item expires, it may be absorbed into the space, reused in a nother project, given away, or sold at the electronics flea market.
