# Goo Dog Printer

## Safety and Certification

Resin 3D print certification required.

## Hazards

**!!! DANGER !!!**

- **Resin is toxic and an irritant.** Gloves must be worn when handling uncured resin or IPA.
- The resin can produce fumes that are harmful, and the IPA will quickly evaporate into the air.
- Make sure the space is being ventilated or substances are minimally exposed to air.
- Electrical hazard, be careful for pacemakers or other electrical hazards.
- Moving robot, be weary of pinching.

### Prohibited Operations

- **Do not** hack or damage equipment in the name of repairs.
- **Do not** leave printer hood open.
- **Do not** place anything (besides a glass stirring rod) in or allow IPA-wet objects to contaminate the resin vat.

## Pre-flight checklist

- Check resin level of vat. Do not fill above MAX line.
- Make sure wanted resin color is present.
- Make sure proper machine is selected in slicer.
- Make sure build plate is clean and dry.
- Make sure there is no partially-cured object stuck to the vat release film.
- Replace wash station IPA if too cloudy with resin.

## Operation checklist

Listen for sounds of raft layers detaching from vat release film. This is only necessary for the first couple of minutes, but pausing the print after 15 min or so and checking for proper adhesion is a good practice.
(This is typically not an issue but has been once or twice)

## Post-flight checklist

- Place sheet of aluminum foil on the white plastic tray and move entire build plate onto the foil. Raise the printer cover and wedge the plastic tray against the resin vat to protect against uncured resin dripping onto the shelf.
- Scrape the completed prints off the plate and spray models lightly with IPA from squeeze bottle.
- Use gloved fingers/snips to remove supports from models (can be done after curing, your call).
- Remove cover of wash station and basket and place basket on top of the cover on a flat surface.
- Take models and put them in the basket, then return basket to wash station.
- Wash models for minimum of 5 minutes.
- Remove models from wash station and place on shop towel. Models should dry completely before curing.
- Place dried models in Cure station and start cure process. 2 minutes is a good baseline for tabletop minis, up to 10 minutes for parts larger than 3cm in solid diameter.
